What are the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) and when were they created?\nAnswer: The Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) are a set of 17 goals created in the year 2015 by leaders from 193 countries to address global challenges such as poverty, hunger, climate change, and inequality.\n--------------------------------------------------\n\nQuestion: 2. How has access to electricity changed between 1990 and 2010, and what are the implications for the future?\nAnswer: Between 1990 and 2010, the number of people with access to electricity increased by 1.7 billion. This progress is significant and shows that more people have been connected to electricity during that time period. However, as the world's population continues to rise, there will be a growing need for cheap energy to meet the demands of lighting homes, using electronic devices, and conducting daily activities. The challenge lies in ensuring that this energy is sourced sustainably to avoid further negative impacts on the environment, such as those caused by fossil fuels and greenhouse gas emissions. Therefore, investing in clean energy sources like solar and wind power is crucial to meeting electricity needs while protecting the environment for the future.\n--------------------------------------------------\n\nQuestion: 6.
